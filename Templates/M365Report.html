Microsoft Secure Score

[~SecureScoreValues~]
Secure Score is a measure, provided by Microsoft, of an organization's security posture, The lower the number, the more actions that need to be taken to address security concerns in the Microsoft 365 tenant.

Multifactor Authentication Status (MFA)

[~MFAStatusDescription~]
Multi-factor authentication is a process where a user is prompted during the initial sign-in process for an additional form of identification, vastly increasing account security. It is considered a security baseline. Without it, your users' accounts are at much greater risk of being compromised.

Modern Authentication

[~ModernAuthDescription~]
Legacy (as opposed to Modern) sign-ins are an indication that obsolete or insecure methods are being used to access your company data. Common examples include the way mobile phones used to have email configured on them, or the way scan to email on a photocopier was configured. These older protocols are often what malicious actors used to get access to your data, and they should be disabled.

User Activity

[~UserActivityDescription~]
Inactive accounts are accounts that have not been signed in to for 90 days. They should be removed from your directory if they are no longer needed, to maintain data integrity, and to remove the security risk they pose (inactive accounts can allow previous employees access to data they are no longer authorised to access, and they also provide a potential target for hackers).

Email Security

This is a list of all email enabled domain names in your Microsoft 365 tenant. It is important they all have valid SPF and DKIM DNS records to prevent spoofing of your domain. Without these protections, it is easier for a malicious actor to pose as a representative of your company when sending an email. As an example, this is often used to forge email invoices that include incorrect bank details, with the intention of intercepting large sums of money.

This is a summary of sign-in information for your Microsoft 365 tenant over the last 30 days. It shows the countries where sign-in attempts are occurring from, and which which ones were successful.
