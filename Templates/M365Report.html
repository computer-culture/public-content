Security

Microsoft Secure Score

[~SecureScorePercent~]
[~SecureScoreValues~]
Secure Score is a measure provided by Microsoft of an organisation's security posture (the overall cybersecurity strength). The higher the number the better. The lower the number, the more actions that need to be taken to address security concerns in the Microsoft 365 tenant.

Multifactor Authentication Status (MFA)

[~MFAStatusDescription~]
Multi-factor authentication is a process where a user is prompted during the initial sign-in process for an additional form of identification. By adding MFA account security is vastly increased. It is considered a security baseline and without it, your users' accounts are at much greater risk of being compromised.

Modern Authentication

[~ModernAuthDescription~]
Legacy (as opposed to Modern) sign-ins are an indication that obsolete or insecure methods are being used to access your company data. Common examples include the way mobile phones used to have email configured on them, or the way 'scan to email' on a photocopier was configured. These older protocols are often what malicious actors use to get access to your data and should be disabled.

Inactive Accounts

[~UserActivityDescription~]
Inactive accounts are Microsoft 365 user accounts that have not been signed in to for 90 days. Inactive accounts should be removed from your directory if they are no longer needed. Removing these accounts helps maintain data integrity, removes the risk of allowing previous employees access to now unauthorised data, and limits target accounts for hackers to exploit. Any inactive accounts present are listed later in the report.

Email Security

This is a list of all email enabled domain names in your Microsoft 365 tenant. It is important they all have valid SPF and DKIM DNS records to prevent spoofing of your domain. Without these protections, it is easier for a malicious actor to pose as a representative of your company when sending an email. As an example, this is often used to forge email invoices that include incorrect bank details, with the intention of intercepting large sums of money.
